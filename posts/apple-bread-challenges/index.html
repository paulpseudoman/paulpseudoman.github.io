Problem: A random walk on $\mathbb{Z}^2$, can be thought of as a position of particle, at time $n$, denoted by $S_n$ which is defined inductively below. Let $x \in \mathbb{Z}^2$,$n \geq 1$.

  • Let $S_0 = x$
  • Suppose we have defined $S_1,S_2,…S_{n−1}$.
  • At time $n$, the particle chooses one of the four neighbors of the vertex $S_{n−1}$ at random and moves to that neighbor which we denote by $S_n$. Simulate and plot the range of the random walk on $\mathbb{Z}^2$ for:

(a) $n = 10$ and $x =0$ (origin)

(b) $n =100$ and $x =0$ (origin)

(c) $x = 0$ and till the first time the walk hits a point at a distance $55$ from the origin.

(d) $x = (55,0)$ and till the first time the walk hits the origin.

Challenge 2: Link to heading

Problem: We shall simulate and understand the One-Dimensional Annihilation-Creation, ${An}_{n\geq 1}$. We describe the model below:

(a) Let $A_0 = {0}$(i.e. 0 is occupied) and fix some integer $K\geq 2$.

(b) Given $A_n$, (i.e. finite sites occupied by at time $n$)the set $A_{n+1}$ is constructed by first adding $K$ sites one by one and then deleting $K-1$ sites one by one. More precisely,

(i) To add a particle, its new site is chosen uniformly from the boundary of the given set of occupied sites, (a site $y$ belongs to the boundary $\partial a$ of a set $a \subset Z$ if $y \in \mathbb{Z}\setminus a$ and if there exists $x\in a$ with $|x-y| = 1$).

(ii) To delete a particle, a site is chosen uniformly from the set of all occupied sites.

  1. How much does An grow at each step ?

  2. Can you write an R that simulates the process $A_n$ at each time n ? Using gganimate (or otherwise), can you produce a dynamic simulation of the process over $n$ for say $1000$ steps with $K = 3$ ?

  3. A gap of $A \subset \mathbb{Z}$ is defined as a nonempty subset $g$ of $\mathbb{Z}\setminus A$ with the property that there exist $x_1, x_2 \in A$ with $g = {y \in \mathbf{Z} : x_1 < y < x_2}$. Let $G(A)$ denote the number of gaps of $A$, let $G^{(2)}(A)$ denote the number of gaps with $|g|\geq 2$ and let $L(A)$ denote the number of unoccupied sites between the endpoints of $A$, i.e., $L(A) = |g_1|+…+|g_{G(A)}|$, where $g_1,…,g_{G(A)}$ are the different gaps of $A$.

(Step 1) For each $n =1,…1000$,simulate $A_n$ and compute $L(A_n)$, $G^{(2)}(A_n)$

(Step 2) Replicate Step 1, 100 times and set $L_n$ be the average of $L(A_n)$ over the 100 trials for each $n$ and $G^{(2)}_n$ be the average of $G^{(2)}(A_n)$ over the 100 trials for each $n$.

(Step 3) Decide if it is likely that $L_n = \mathcal{O}(h_n)$ where $h(n) = \frac{\log{n}}{\log{\log{n}}}$

(Step 4) Plot $G^{(2)}_n$.
